# 10 — Implementation Timeline & Scope

**Source:** IRBM e-Invoice General Guideline v4.7 (published 7 July 2026)

This section answers the first question anyone asks: **am I in scope, and
when do I have to comply?**

## The mandatory rollout schedule

Malaysia rolls out e-Invoice in phases based on annual turnover / revenue:

| Phase | Targeted taxpayers | Mandatory date |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Annual turnover / revenue **> RM 100 million** | **1 August 2024** |
| 2 | Annual turnover / revenue **> RM 25 million and ≤ RM 100 million** | **1 January 2025** |
| 3 | Annual turnover / revenue **> RM 5 million and ≤ RM 25 million** | **1 July 2025** |
| 4 | Annual turnover / revenue **up to RM 5 million** | **1 January 2026** |

All phases are **live** as of this writing (April 2026). If you're reading this
and haven't implemented yet, you are either exempt (see below) or overdue.

## How your turnover is determined

IRBM uses a fixed reference point — **financial year 2022** — regardless of
what your revenue has done since:

1. **Taxpayers with audited financial statements:** annual turnover / revenue
   from the **statement of comprehensive income in your FY2022 audited
   financial statements**.
2. **Taxpayers without audited financial statements:** **annual revenue
   reported in the YA2022 tax return**.
3. **Change of accounting year end in FY2022:** pro-rate to 12 months.
   *Example: if your new FY2022 covers 18 months at RM 60 million, your
   pro-rated annual turnover = RM 60M × 12/18 = RM 40M → falls in Phase 2
   (1 Jan 2025 deadline).*

Once your bracket is determined, **subsequent changes in turnover don't
change your deadline**. If you were in the RM100M+ bracket in FY2022 but your
FY2024 revenue dropped to RM 50M, you still had the 1 Aug 2024 deadline.

## New businesses (commencing 2023 onwards)

The rules are slightly different for businesses that didn't exist in 2022:

| Business commencement year | Rule |
|---|---|
| **2023 to 2025** with annual turnover / revenue ≥ **RM 1,000,000** | Mandatory from **1 July 2026** |
| **2026 onwards** | Mandatory from **1 July 2026** or the operation commencement date, whichever is later |
| **2026 onwards**, if first year revenue < RM 1,000,000 | Mandatory from **1 January of the second year** following the year you cross RM 1M |

Example: a business that starts in March 2026 with RM 800K first-year revenue
and hits RM 1.2M in 2027 → mandatory from **1 January 2028**.

## Voluntary early adoption

Any taxpayer can **voluntarily** participate in e-Invoice before their
mandatory date, regardless of revenue bracket. Reasons to do so:

- Be ready before buyers / suppliers force your hand
- Work out kinks in sandbox without time pressure
- Gain audit-ready invoice storage early
- Easier onboarding while IRBM support queue is shorter

## Who is scoped in

e-Invoice applies to **all persons in Malaysia**. The General Guideline
enumerates:

1. Association
2. Body of persons
3. Branch
4. Business trust
5. Co-operative societies
6. Corporations
7. Limited liability partnership
8. Partnership
9. Property trust fund
10. Property trust
11. Real estate investment trust
12. Representative office and regional office
13. Trust body
14. Unit trust

All B2B, B2C, and B2G transactions are in scope (B2G flows work like B2B).

## Exemptions — who is NOT required to issue e-Invoice

Per Section 1.6 of the General Guideline v4.7, these persons are **currently
exempted** from issuing e-Invoice (including self-billed e-Invoice):

### Entity / person exemptions

| Exempted party | Conditions |
|---|---|
| **Foreign diplomatic office** | Always exempt |
| **Individual not conducting business** | i.e. personal, non-business activity |
| **Statutory body, statutory authority, local authority** | Exempt only for: (i) payment/fee/charge/statutory levy/summon/compound/penalty collection in carrying out functions under any written law; and (ii) goods sold / services performed **before 1 July 2025** |
| **International organisations** (see list below) | Exempt for transactions of goods sold / services performed **before 1 July 2025** |
| **Taxpayers with annual turnover / revenue < RM 1,000,000** | Always exempt |

### Transaction-type exemptions

These **transactions** don't require an e-Invoice even if the parties
otherwise would be required to issue one:

| Transaction type | Notes |
|---|---|
| Employment income | Covered by standard payroll / PCB |
| Pension | |
| Alimony | |
| Distribution of dividend | In specific circumstances (see Section 11 of e-Invoice Specific Guideline) |
| Zakat | |
| Securities / derivatives traded on a stock exchange or derivatives exchange (Malaysia or elsewhere) | Contract value |
| Disposal of shares in unlisted companies | **Except** where the disposer is a company, LLP, trust body, or co-operative society (those still need e-Invoice) |
| Donations / contributions received | Per Question 1, Part A of the e-Invoice FAQs for Donations or Contributions |

### Important caveats

- **Suppliers to exempt parties still have to issue e-Invoice.** If you sell
  to an embassy, you still need an e-Invoice. You can replace the Buyer's
  details per Table 3.5 of the Specific Guideline.
- **Entities owned by exempt persons are NOT exempt.** The exemption is for
  the person themselves. Their companies / LLPs still have to implement
  e-Invoice on the normal timeline.
- **Exempt parties can voluntarily implement** e-Invoice to support
  digitalization.
- **The exemption list is reviewed and updated periodically** — don't assume
  it's static.

## Transition period practicalities

During the rollout, expect a mixed environment:

- Suppliers on earlier phases → already issuing validated e-Invoices
- Suppliers on later phases → still issuing normal receipts
- **You receive whatever your supplier issues** — no obligation to reject
  pre-e-Invoice receipts from suppliers not yet in their mandatory phase

Your **issuance** obligation starts on your mandatory date. Your
**receiving** obligation (accepting e-Invoices from your suppliers) is
continuous from when your suppliers start issuing them.

## 2022 audited financials: the edge case

If your FY2022 audited financial statements show exactly RM 5,000,000 (or
exactly any threshold boundary), you fall in the **higher** bracket. The
thresholds are strictly "greater than" / "up to" (inclusive of the upper
bound). Read the table again carefully if you're near a boundary.

## Special Voluntary Disclosure Programme (SVDP)

> **NEW — added in Specific Guideline v4.8 (7 July 2026), Section 17, pp.123–126.**

IRBM launched a penalty-free amnesty window for taxpayers to catch up on
missed or non-compliant e-Invoices:

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Window** | 7 July 2026 – 31 December 2027 |
| **Who qualifies** | Any taxpayer who missed, partially submitted, or submitted non-compliant e-Invoices since their mandatory date; including those already under compliance review |
| **Version tags** | Must use **"SVDP 1.2"** (no digital signature) or **"SVDP 1.3"** (with digital signature) — not for regular submissions |
| **Consolidated catch-up** | Must submit **month-by-month**, not lumped across months |
| **Protection** | No compliance reviews, penalties, or prosecution on disclosed e-Invoices |
| **Exceptions** | Does NOT protect fraud, wilful default, negligence, or submissions that themselves don't comply |

This is especially relevant for **Phase 4 taxpayers** (up to RM5M / new
businesses from 1 July 2026) who are only now starting, as well as earlier-phase
taxpayers who have gaps.

For full details, worked examples, and rules, see
[Specific Guideline v4.8 — Section 17](../11-irbm-specific-guideline/README.md#17-e-invoice-special-voluntary-disclosure-programme-svdp).

## Practical take

If you're reading this in 2026 and don't know your mandatory date, **you are
almost certainly past it**. Contact your accountant or IRBM to confirm.
The latest voluntary-adoption path is for businesses that haven't crossed
RM 1M yet. If you have gaps in your e-Invoice compliance, the **SVDP
window (7 July 2026 – 31 December 2027)** lets you catch up without
penalty — see the section above.
